I moved to Atlanta in 2011 to pursue a BS in Aerospace Engineering at the Georgia Institute if Technology and soon took a position in the Center for Space Systems. I was assigned to the Prox-1 Nanosatellite, check it out.

Serving as the thermal subsystem lead, I worked with a few fellow undergrads to make sure the satellite would maintain operable temperatures during flight. The work included extensive thermal modelling using Siemens NX 8 as well as Arduino programming for active heating elements.

I also had the privilege of working at the Ben Zinn Combustion Lab performing flame tests on silane. Blowing stuff up all day is just as fun as it sounds.
